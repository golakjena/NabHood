NABHOOD Offers Free Legal Advice To Members

Miller Law Group, P.C, Cazeau, Deanne L. Ayers-Howard; Stokes Lazarus & Carmichael LLP; The Goldstein Law Group, P.C.; Ricky Patel and Powell Goldstein LLP, in conjunction with NABHOOD, are providing limited legal consultation regarding any matter that may arise in your business. As a NABHOOD member, you are entitled to up to three thirty-minute consultations per calendar year with a firm's attorney by telephone or Internet e-mail on routine matters at no charge to you. These consults will be general in nature to point a member in right direction to raise issues for the member to consider. These issues can involve organization of partnerships, corporations, limited liability companies, joint ventures, agreements among partners, shareholders or members of a LLC, as well as any other agreements related to the business venture. Additionally, and perhaps most of all, attorneys can assist in issues pertaining to the franchisor/franchisee relationship such as negotiating franchise agreements, recommended franchise provisions and litigation support including document review and limited legal research. To activate this service, please contact an attorney listed below.

Miller Law Group, P.C, Cazeau, Deanne L. Ayers-Howard; Stokes Lazarus & Carmichael LLP; The Goldstein Law Group, P.C.; Ricky Patel and Powell Goldstein LLP are full-service business firms representing clients throughout the country in all phases of their business dealings. These firms traditionally represent hospitality employers and have extensive experience in the hospitality industry, including franchise law, hospitality employment law, convention contracts, real estate and collections. NABHOOD will be expanding the law firms that offer free legal service, ultimately to six attorneys from different parts of the country. NABHOOD has negotiated this benefit for its members and makes no representation of any sort, including but not limited to, quality of work or legal costs. Members do not have an attorney-client relationship until a formal agreement has been entered into in writing between attorney and the member.

Please visit the attorney's Web sites (listed below) for useful articles.
